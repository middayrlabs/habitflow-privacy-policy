Privacy Policy for HabitFlow

Effective Date: May 1, 2026

At HabitFlow, your privacy is a core principle, not an afterthought. This policy explains in plain language what data we handle, how third-party services interact with the app, and what rights you have. HabitFlow is designed as an offline-first application — your habit data never leaves your device.

Section 01

Information We Do Not Collect

HabitFlow does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. Specifically, we do not collect:

  • Name or email address
  • Account credentials or authentication data
  • Health, fitness, or behavioral data entered into the app

HabitFlow itself does not collect or transmit usage analytics, crash reports, or telemetry to our own servers. However, third-party services such as Google AdMob may collect limited usage and interaction data for advertising purposes.

HabitFlow does not collect personal identifiers such as your name, email address, or contact details. However, third-party services like Google AdMob may use device-level identifiers such as the Advertising ID for ad targeting and measurement purposes.

All habit data, streaks, and settings are stored locally on your device only. Uninstalling the app removes all associated data.
Section 02

Third-Party Services (AdMob)

HabitFlow displays advertisements served by Google AdMob, a third-party advertising platform operated by Google LLC. To serve relevant ads, AdMob may collect certain non-personal and device-level information, including:

  • Advertising ID (resettable identifier assigned by your device OS)
  • General device information (model, OS version)
  • Approximate location (country/region level, if permitted)
  • Ad interaction data (impressions, clicks)

This data is considered non-personal and is used for advertising and performance purposes. It is collected and processed by Google under their own privacy policy. HabitFlow has no access to or control over the data AdMob collects. You can review Google's data practices and opt-out options at:

https://policies.google.com/privacy →

You can reset or limit your Advertising ID at any time in your device's Settings under Privacy or Ads.

Section 03

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

HabitFlow itself does not use cookies, web beacons, pixel tags, or any tracking technologies.

Google AdMob may use cookies or similar technologies within its ad-serving SDK to measure ad performance and deliver personalized or contextual advertisements. These technologies are governed exclusively by Google's policies.

If you prefer non-personalized ads, you can opt out of interest-based advertising through your device's advertising settings or via adssettings.google.com.

Section 04

Data Security

Because HabitFlow stores all data locally on your device, your information is protected by your device's built-in security mechanisms — including screen lock, encryption (if enabled), and operating system sandboxing.

We strongly recommend keeping your device updated and using a secure lock screen to safeguard your data. Since we operate no servers storing your data, there is no central database that could be compromised.

Section 05

Children's Privacy

HabitFlow is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided personal data through the app, please contact us at the email below and we will take appropriate action. Please note that ad content shown via AdMob is subject to Google's own child-safety policies.

Section 06

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, legal requirements, or third-party service integrations.

When we make changes, we will update the Effective Date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify you within the app. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of HabitFlow after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
